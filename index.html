What is the impact of sleep deprivation?

Ever tried to teach a tired child anything?

Push and pull between amygdala (anger, irritability, anxiety, fear) and neocortex (consciousness, attention, perception, reasoning)

Won't be surprised to learn that memory, manual dexterity & complex decision making are the first affected brain regions - parts of the brain actually fall asleep at different times

Mitochondria are tiny structure that fuel the body by producing molecules called adenosine triphosphate or ATP...

Long term effects of lack of sleep involve raised bood pressure/heart problems, but short term sleep deprivation can impact on our memories

David Eagleman references a study that attempted to teach retrograde amnesiacs how to play tetris.

SW sleep preserves our memories by recapitulating what we have learned during the day. REM incorporates these memories into what we already know.

...we still don't fully understand the reasons/implications of sleep - elephants 4 hrs, big cats 15 hrs, humans 8 hrs, brown bat 19 hrs

But we know it plays a number of key roles, and without it your brain will struggle to fully function!

The wandering mind

I find that when I am struggling with a problem not only is it energy consuming, but one of the side-effects is lack of focus or "mind-wandering"

Your mind is like a town - when focused everyone is in the town square but when wandering, activity is distributed. Default mode network.

Benefits of mindfulness are well documented. + shrunken amygdala + enlarged prefrontal cortex. During mind wandering, subjects report less happy than not.

Ever daydreamed through a green light or asked someone for the time only to have zoned out by the time they tell you the answer? So why do we do it and how could it help?

Advantages of mind wandering include:

  • attentional cycling - honing mental juggling skills - working on different problems simultaneously
  • dishabituation - refreshed attention, brain responds to change - change of mental scenery
  • relief from boredom - ability to persevere with tedious but necessary tasks
  • also, problem incubation - where your brain is able to work asynchronously on a problem without direct input from your consciousness

Unusual Use Tasks

In order to explore the potential impact of mind wandering on creative problem solving, Baird & Smallwood conducted a study involving Unusual Use Tasks

Their hypothesis was that tasks without a working memory load elicits more mind wandering than tasks with a working memory load...

pencil - chopstick, lock pick, javelin, rolling pin,

Problem incubation where freeing up your working memory allows it to work unhindered and autonomously on the problem that the conscious brain provided it.

So got get that cup of tea, or find a chore to do or let your mind wander off because it all helps!

Ego depletion

Source: Electronic Arts Inc. (2019). The Sims Video Games - Official EA Site.

All forms of voluntary attention and effort (cognitive, emotional & physical) draw at least partly on a pool of shared mental energy

Successive tasks where you force yourself to do something mean that you become increasingly unwilling to exert additional self-control

This phenomenon is called ego depletion

Situations that deplete you are varied but generally involve conflict and the need to suppress a natural tendency

  • e.g. responding kindly to bad behaviour or suppressing a natural emotional response - maybe in the work place
  • e.g. making a series of choices each of which involve conflict e.g. choosing what to have for lunch ;)
  • e.g. trying to impress people - suppressing your personality, overriding your own emotions

The nervous system consumes more glucose than most other parts of the body, and effortful mental activity appears to especially expensive in the currency of glucose

The flawed story

A flawed story is one where the ending does not logically follow from the premise and events

Sometimes flawed stories delight us - magic! But not usually in programming.

Why is story so important?

In Yuval Noah Harari's book - Sapiens - he talks about an individual being able to "keep up" with ~150 other people, and posits that we are only able to successfully cooperate as a species through the means of shared fiction

We are built for story, it is in everything we do, think and want. A healthy mind is the "central protagonist" in their own hero epic

Rubber duck debugging

Play to your brain's strengths:

  • Speak/write your problem out, because by sequencing it you are removing it from your RAM and ordering it. Can see your own flaws. Rubber duck debugging!
  • Also, by speaking you are hearing and by writing you are seeing. Brain is like a city.

Challenge your biases

When we learn about a problem space we start from a place of questioning...

Questions > beliefs > hypotheses > problem paradigm model > once established/complex we start to ignore/forget/discredit inconsistent information

We operate on models - they are shortcuts that allow us to progress through life efficiently - but we are flawed and so are our models

Often when we feel stuck it is because we are blind to the incorrect information we have encoded. Seek an open mind
